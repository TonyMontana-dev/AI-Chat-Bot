National Veterans and Military Families Month is a time dedicated to the brave
men and women who have served our nation, and the resilient families who stand
by their side. Throughout this special month, Veterans Services is excited to
bring you a series of events and activities that aim to celebrate, support, and
honor our veterans and military families.

Yellow ribbons will line the campus perimeter to remember all who have served in
war and peacetime.


EVENTS


COLLECTION DRIVE FOR OPERATION SUPPORT OUR TROOPS

Wednesday, Nov. 1 through Monday, Dec. 4
Veterans Services, Student Services Center (SSC), Room 2225

In collaboration with COD Cares Gift of Giving, Veterans Services will be
collecting OSOT’s most needed items for our deployed military members. Drop-off
bins will be in the Veterans Services Office, SSC 2225, the TEC main entrance in
front of TEC 1034, the HSC lobby, and near the entrance of the Bookstore.

 * Most Needed Items
   [https://www.osotamerica.org/comfort-and-care-package-program/holiday-stocking-program/]


CAREER SERVICES WEBINAR: CAREER RESEARCH USING YOUR MOS

Wednesday, Nov. 1 at noon

Choosing a career can be challenging however, learning how and where to research
can help. This webinar will cover several valuable websites, including Career
Coach, where you can explore career summaries, local wages, employment
projections, and other helpful information to explore potential career paths.
Additionally, it will be demonstrated how veterans can use their MOS code to
identify civilian equivalent job titles.

Register to receive the webinar link.
[https://www.canva.com/design/DAEdu_SMOFU/ci-EbMK_XZXvdbVPZgDGRg/view?website#2]


VETERANS DAY CEREMONY

Thursday, Nov. 9, 9 to 9:30 a.m.
Flagpole outside Student Services Center (SSC), Door 1

 * Presentation of Colors, DuPage Honor Guard
 * National Anthem, Erick Flores
 * Speaker, Jason Ertz, College of DuPage Reference Librarian/Professor, Marine
   Corps Veteran
 * Light refreshments will be served in the Veterans Lounge, Student Services
   Center (SSC), Room 3260 following the ceremony.

In the event of inclement weather, the ceremony will be held in the Student
Services Center (SSC), Atrium.


GREETING CARDS FOR VETERANS AND DEPLOYED MILITARY MEMBERS

Thursday, Nov. 9, 11 a.m. to 1 p.m.
Student Service Center (SSC), Atrium

Write notes of thanks and encouragement to veterans and deployed military
members to show your appreciation and support. Co-sponsored by Gift of Giving.


ARMED FORCES EXHIBITION

Thursday, Nov. 16, 10 p.m. to 3 p.m.
Student Services Center (SSC), Atrium

Armed forces memorabilia: service uniforms, branch insignia, awards, medals and
ribbons.


VETERANS DAY READ-IN

Thursday, Nov. 16, noon to 1:30 p.m.
Library Alcove, Student Resource Center (SRC), Room 2020

Service and sacrifice of veterans and their families are honored through diverse
viewpoints reflections and presentations. Hosted by College of DuPage English
Professor Robert Hazard.


HEALING HIDDEN WOUNDS OF WAR

Tuesday, Nov. 28, 10 a.m. to 1 p.m.
Student Services Center (SSC), Room 3245

 * 10 a.m. to noon, Mental Health Resource Fair
 * Noon, Brown Bag Mental Health Panel Discussion

This event is dedicated to addressing the critical topic of mental health within
the veteran community. Explore a comprehensive mental health fair featuring
resource tables offering informative materials about their services. Experience
the therapeutic comfort of therapy dogs as well as self-care breaks throughout
the event. Bring your lunch and engage in a thought-provoking panel discussion
with veterans who will share their personal journeys and insight on mental
health. 

This event will be co-sponsored by the Student Veterans Association.


CAREER SERVICES WEBINAR: MAKING A CIVILIAN RESUME WITH MILITARY EXPERIENCE

Wednesday, Nov. 29, noon


While there is no "one size fits all" resume, there are a number of universal
tips and guidelines that we like to share with students. Join our webinar to
learn about options for getting started and strategies you can take to feel
confident in the document you create. During this session military examples will
be used and how to present the information in a civilian job search. Bring your
questions for this helpful resume discussion. 

Register to receive the webinar link.
[https://www.canva.com/design/DAEdu_SMOFU/ci-EbMK_XZXvdbVPZgDGRg/view?website#2]
