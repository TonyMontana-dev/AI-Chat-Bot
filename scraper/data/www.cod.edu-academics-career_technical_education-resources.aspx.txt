The Strengthening Career and Technical Education for the 21st Century Act
(Perkins V) has identified special population groups
[/admission/special-populations.aspx]who have been historically underserved.
This list includes College of DuPage and local area resources, divided into
special population groups for easier access.

For a more comprehensive list of additional local area resources,
visit 211dupage.gov [https://211dupage.gov/] or call 211.

Economically Disadvantaged
 * College of DuPage Fuel Pantry [/student_life/pantry/index.aspx]: Provides
   food for members of the campus community who may be food insecure.
 * College of DuPage Office of Financial Aid [/costs/index.aspx]: Provides a
   range of services that help bridge the gap between the financial resources of
   students and their families and the cost of education. 
 * Glen Ellyn Walk-In Ministry: [https://www.geumc.org/] Services include
   Automobile Insurance Payment Assistance, Automotive Repair and Maintenance,
   Food Vouchers, Gas Money, Prescription Expense Assistance, Transportation
   Expense Assistance, Utility Service Payment Assistance. 
 * Hope’s Front Door [http://www.hopesfrontdoor.com/]: Provides assistance with
   public benefit applications, budget counseling, food, transportation expense,
   clothing, employment support services, and the coordination of access to
   prescription and health care services. 
 * Illinois Department of Human Services
   [https://www.dhs.state.il.us/page.aspx]: Processes applications and
   interviews for the state funded medical, food, and cash assistance programs.
 * Peoples Resource Center [https://www.peoplesrc.org/]: Services include Adult
   Basic Education, Arts and Crafts Instruction, Computer and Related Technology
   Classes, Employment Preparation, Food Pantries, Food Stamps/SNAP
   Applications, Job Finding Assistance, Rental Deposit Assistance, Rent Payment
   Assistance. 

Individuals with Disabilities
 * College of DuPage Center for Access and Accommodations
   [/student_life/resources/access-accommodations/index.aspx]: Provides equality
   of education opportunities for eligible students with disabilities including
   access, accommodations, and coordination of support services.
 * AbilityLinks [https://abilitylinks.org/]: Online searchable database of
   employers and job opportunities, programs, and services for persons with
   disabilities.
 * Illinois Division of Rehabilitation Services
   [https://www.dhs.state.il.us/page.aspx?item=29764]: Assists people with
   disabilities and their families with making informed choices in employment,
   education, and independent living opportunities.
 * PACE DuPage Access Program [https://www.pacebus.com/dupage-uber]: Pace's
   pilot program for ADA Paratransit riders in DuPage County offers an Uber ride
   of up to $30 at no cost to the rider.
 * Ray Graham Association [https://www.raygraham.org/]: Provides services to
   those with disabilities through residential, education, supported employment,
   behavioral, physical, speech and occupational therapies, behavioral
   intervention/counseling, transportation assistance, and respite care
   programs.

Individuals Preparing for Non-Traditional Fields
 * National Alliance for Partnerships in Equity [https://napequity.org/]:
   Fundamental to NAPE’s mission is the development of education and workforce
   development programs that expand career opportunities for men and women,
   especially in high-skill, high-wage nontraditional career fields.
 * WorkNet DuPage Career Center [https://www.worknetdupage.org/]: Provides
   various employment, career, and job search services to job seekers and
   business in DuPage County.

Single Parents, Including Single Pregnant Women
 * YWCA Child Care Assistance Program
   [https://ywcachicago.org/our-work/family-support-services/child-care-assistance-program/]:
   Manages the state funded Child Care Assistance program for DuPage and Kane
   Counties to help low-income families pay for childcare. 

Currently Unemployed
 * College of DuPage Career Services
   [/student_life/resources/career-services/index.aspx]: Offers a variety of job
   search resources for students, alumni, and community residents. 
 * Career and Networking Center [https://careernetworkingcenter.org/]: Offers
   job coaching on Career Exploration, Job Search Strategy, Resume Review,
   Networking, Interviewing practice, and LinkedIn support. 
 * Goodwill Workforce Connection Center
   [https://www.goodwillchicago.com/help-for-employers/workforce-connection-centers]:
   Offers job search assistance including resume and cover letter assistance,
   job postings, a chance to meet with local employers who are hiring, workshops
   on resumes, interviewing, and basic computer skills, computer and internet
   access and telephone, fax, and email access. 
 * Illinois Department of Employment Security [https://ides.illinois.gov/]:
   Services include State Unemployment Insurance, Veteran Employment Programs. 
 * Peoples Resource Cente [https://www.peoplesrc.org/]r: Services include Adult
   Basic Education, Arts and Crafts Instruction, Computer and Related Technology
   Classes, Employment Preparation, Food Pantries, Food Stamps/SNAP
   Applications, Job Finding Assistance, Rental Deposit Assistance, Rent Payment
   Assistance. 
 * Poised for Success [https://www.poisedforsuccess.org/]: Provides, at no
   charge, interview and work appropriate clothing to women, on assistance, in
   the job market, by way of an agency referral program. 
 * WorkNet DuPage Career Center [https://www.worknetdupage.org/]: Provides
   various employment, career, and job search services to job seekers and
   business in DuPage County. 

Homeless Individuals
 * College of DuPage Office of Financial Aid [/costs/index.aspx]: Can assist
   students with homeless status apply for financial aid.
 * 360 Youth Services
   [https://360youthservices.org/housing/#transitional-housing]: Provides
   emergency shelter, transitional and rapid rehousing to youth of all gender
   identities and sexual orientations. 
 * DuPage Pads [https://dupagepads.org/what-we-do/interim-housing-center/]:
   Provides nightly emergency shelter and meals at multiple locations. 
 * Midwest Shelter for Homeless Veterans [https://www.helpaveteran.org/]:
   Provides transitional housing and supportive services for single male
   Veterans. 
 * Outreach Community Ministries-Wheaton Youth Outreach
   [https://weareoutreach.org/locations/outreach-wheaton/]: Provides
   transitional housing programs for young women and housing expense assistance
   for those who either are homeless or soon will be. 

English Language Learners
 * College of DuPage English Language Acquisition
   [/academics/continuing-education/adult-education/ela.aspx]: Provides free
   English Language Acquisition classes to develop conversation, listening,
   speaking, reading, and writing skills. 
 * World Relief DuPage [https://worldrelief.org/chicagoland/]: English as a
   Second Language, Immigrant/Refugee Employment Programs,
   Immigration/Naturalization Legal Services, Information and Referral, Life
   Skills Education, Refugee Resettlement Services.

Youth Who Are in or Have Aged Out of the Foster Care System
 * College of DuPage Counseling, Advising, and Transfer Services
   [/student_life/resources/counseling/index.aspx]: Provides academic advising,
   short-term mental health counseling, and career counseling to ensure student
   success. 
 * 360 Youth Services
   [https://360youthservices.org/housing/#transitional-housing]: Provides
   emergency shelter, transitional and rapid rehousing to youth of all gender
   identities and sexual orientations. 
 * Child Welfare Information Gateway
   [https://www.childwelfare.gov/topics/outofhome/independent/resources/]:
   Provides resources for youth aging out of home care and transition to
   adulthood. 
 * Illinois Department of Children and Family Services
   [https://dcfs.illinois.gov/brighter-futures/independence.html]: Promoting
   independence and self-sufficiency for young people who have spent time in the
   child welfare system. 
 * Office of the Cook County Public Guardian [https://www.publicguardian.org/]:
   Assistance to current and former DCFS wards in order to achieve
   independence. 

Youth With Parents in Active Military And/or on Active Duty
 * College of DuPage Veterans Services
   [/student_life/resources/veterans/index.aspx]: Provide services and supports
   for Veteran students and their families. 
 * Illinois Joining Forces [https://www.illinoisjoiningforces.org/]: Connects
   military service members, veterans and their families with community
   resources through their Care Coordination Center. 
 * eBenefits [https://www.ebenefits.va.gov/ebenefits/homepage]: Provides Service
   Members, Veterans, their families, and authorized caregivers with a single
   sign-on, central access point to benefits information online, including
   pensions, education, housing and healthcare benefits.
