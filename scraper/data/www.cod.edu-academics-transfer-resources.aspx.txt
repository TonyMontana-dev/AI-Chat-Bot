Deciding on college or university to finish your studies is an important
decision. To assist you in this process we have collected the following
resources. These links are not a part of College of DuPage and are provided as a
service and no endorsement is expressed or implied.


COLLEGE OF DUPAGE RESOURCES

 * Transfer Student Toolkit
   [https://bxro44g.sharing.bublup.com/mybublup/#/mystuff/001-f-adacf310-4463-40d7-9368-5fe3473a5c13/mixed?lid=001-si-lCyl5pj-I8Wb]:
   Provides a wealth of internal and external resources to assist you in
   planning your transfer.
 * Steps to Transfer [/academics/transfer/transfer-steps.aspx]: This helpful
   guide will help you identify some steps to help ease the transfer process
 * Career Coach
   [/student_life/resources/career-services/electronic-resources/career-coach.aspx]:
   Discover majors and in-demand careers and education based on your interests!
   Take the free Career Assessment to discover where your interests lie in
   connection to transfer majors and programs.
 * Connect with representatives [/academics/transfer/events/index.aspx]: Various
   colleges and universities routinely visit our campus.
 * Check out popular transfer schools [/academics/transfer/colleges/index.aspx]:
   This listing will help you identify some transfer options with some of our
   popular transfer schools. 


ILLINOIS COLLEGES AND UNIVERSITIES TRANSFER RESOURCES

 * Transferology [https://www.transferology.com/school/cod]: This online tool
   will help you identify colleges and universities that will accept your
   transfer courses. Be sure to check out the videos posted on the website to
   learn more about utilizing this tool. COD offers a unique opportunity to
   quickly import your courses. 
 * Transfer Ready [https://transferready.org/]: Website provides a variety of
   resources for students transferring from an Illinois community college to an
   Illinois 4-year college or university.
 * 12 Reasons to stay in Illinois
   [https://www.iacac.org/state-universities-illinois/]: Comprehensives listing
   of majors and minors available at the 12 public Illinois universities and
   admissions guide.
 * Illinois Articulation Initiative (IAI) [http://www.itransfer.org/]: Statewide
   transfer agreement, which is transferable among more than 100 participating
   colleges and universities in Illinois. IAI works best for students who know
   they are going to transfer but are undecided on the college or university
   that will grant their baccalaureate degree.


INSIDE AND OUTSIDE OF ILLINOIS TRANSFER RESOURCES

 * College Scorecard [https://collegescorecard.ed.gov/]: Powered by the US
   Department of Education.  This site allows students to sort and filter search
   results and compare schools based on major, location, typical tuition costs,
   average student loan amount, and graduation rates.  
 * College Board BigFuture© [https://bigfuture.collegeboard.org/]: Know what
   you're interested in? Compare colleges or search for schools based on a
   number of factors such as major, location and cost. Utilize your existing
   College Board account or create a new account to explore BigFuture©
   features. 
 * Historically Black Colleges and Universities (HBCU)
   [http://www.hbcu-central.com/]: List of HBCU’s organized by state.
 * Hispanic Association of Colleges and Universities (HACU)
   [https://www.hacu.net/hacu/default.asp]: HACU represents more than 470
   colleges and universities committed to Hispanic higher education success
 * Phi Theta Kappa Honors Society (PTK): [https://www.ptk.org/] Phi Theta Kappa
   is the only honor society with the distinction of being named the official
   honor society for two-year colleges by the American Association of Community
   Colleges. PTK makes it easy to research and apply for valuable scholarships.
   Learn more about to to join College of DuPage's Phi Beta chapter
   [/student_life/clubs_org/index.aspx]. 
 * U.S. News College Ranking [https://www.usnews.com/best-colleges]: U.S. News'
   annual college ranking. Find out how schools rank based on many different
   criteria such as "best schools in the Midwest," the "best schools for
   business," "schools with the largest number of transfer students."
