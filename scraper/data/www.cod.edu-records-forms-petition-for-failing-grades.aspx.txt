The Academic Regulations Committee reviews all requests for petition of failing
grades. If the failing grade was within the past five years and you meet the
following criteria, the Academic Regulations Committee will consider your
petition for a change of grade, 'F' to 'W' (withdrawal).


PETITION FOR FAILING GRADES CRITERIA

 * Only F grades will be considered 
 * Course was taken fewer than five years ago
 * Explain the circumstances for failed grade(s)
 * Submit supporting documentation via email to gradesdept@cod.edu
   [gradesdept@cod.edu]. Your appeal will be denied if supporting documentation
   is not provided. 
   * Examples of appropriate documentation (suggestions, but not limited to the
     following):
     * Death certificate
     * Funeral services booklet
     * Internet/news article
     * Plane tickets
     * Medical records
     * Court documents/records
     * Letter from doctor, counselor, etc. on letterhead
 * Two petitions per student (you can petition for multiple courses on each
   petition when the circumstances are the same. All grade appeal decisions are
   final.

Refer to Student Appeal Procedures in the Student Services section of the
current College of DuPage Catalog for additional information.  This process
takes approximately eight to 10 weeks. You will be notified in writing of the
result of your appeal.


FORGIVENESS POLICY

If you meet the following criteria, the failing grade(s) will remain on your
transcript but will be removed from your grade point average calculation. There
will be a notation on your official transcript indicating that you have been
granted forgiveness for the failed course(s).

 * Only F grades will be considered
 * A period of at least 36 months of non-enrollment has elapsed since the end of
   the last term of grades to be forgiven (excluding non-credit classes).
 * Upon return, a minimum of 12 consecutive semester hours with no grades of
   'D', 'F', 'S', 'I', or 'X' and no more than two 'W' grades must be earned at
   College of DuPage before forgiveness will be considered. In addition, a
   student must earn the same number of credit hours with a grade of 'C' or
   better equal to the number of credit hours of 'F' grades to be forgiven. 
 * A maximum of 25 quarter hours or 18 semester hours of 1000-level courses and
   above will be forgiven. 
 * Forgiveness will be granted one time only for each student. 

Refer to Standards of Academic Progress in the Academic Information section of
the current College of DuPage Catalog for additional information. This process
takes approximately one week. You will be notified in writing of the result of
your appeal.
